Our Methodology

We evaluated iOS Bitcoin wallets based on security, ease of use, features, and what type of user each wallet best serves. Here's how we approached our analysis:

Security & Custody

We clearly distinguish between self-custody (you hold your keys), custodial (company holds your keys), and collaborative custody (shared control). Each model has trade-offs between convenience and control.

Lightning Network Support

Lightning enables fast, cheap Bitcoin payments. We note which wallets support Lightning natively, as this is increasingly important for everyday Bitcoin use.

Built-In Buying

Some wallets let you buy Bitcoin directly in-app, which is convenient but usually means custodial or KYC requirements. We highlight which wallets offer this and the trade-offs involved.

Availability

Some wallets are restricted to certain countries (like USA only) due to regulatory requirements. We clearly note geographic restrictions so you know what's available to you.
