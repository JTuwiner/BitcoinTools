Credit vs. Debit Cards

This page includes both credit and debit cards. Credit cards typically offer higher rewards rates and sign-up bonuses but require a credit check. Debit cards are linked to your existing funds and may be easier to qualify for—ideal if you prefer not to take on credit or are building credit history.

Important Credit Considerations

  • APRs vary by creditworthiness and issuer—always check current rates before applying
  • Credit requirements: Most rewards cards require good to excellent credit (680+ score)
  • Excluded transactions: Cash advances, balance transfers, and cash-like purchases typically don't earn rewards
  • Tax implications: Crypto rewards may be taxable events depending on your jurisdiction—consult a tax professional
  • Reward volatility: Cryptocurrency rewards can lose value—consider your risk tolerance

Discontinued Crypto Credit Cards

The crypto credit card landscape has changed dramatically since 2020. The "crypto winter" of 2022—marked by the collapse of major exchanges like FTX and lending platforms like BlockFi—wiped out several popular crypto rewards cards. Here's what happened:

Cards That Shut Down

  • BlockFi Rewards Visa Signature Credit Card: BlockFi filed for Chapter 11 bankruptcy in November 2022, just weeks after the FTX collapse. The card offered 1.5% back in Bitcoin and was discontinued—existing cardholders could no longer use their cards.
  • Upgrade Bitcoin Rewards Visa Credit Card: Upgrade shuttered its crypto-earning card in 2023, citing challenging market conditions. The card had offered 1.5% back in Bitcoin on all purchases.

Cards That Removed Crypto Rewards

  • Brex Card: Brex removed cryptocurrency as a redemption option for its corporate card rewards as of August 31, 2024. Cardholders can no longer convert points to crypto.
  • SoFi Unlimited 2% Credit Card: SoFi eliminated the crypto rewards redemption option in early 2023. The card still exists but rewards can no longer be converted to cryptocurrency.

Cards That Never Launched

  • Abra Crypto Card: Abra announced a crypto card on the American Express network in June 2022, but it never materialized. Regulatory challenges and the crypto downturn derailed the launch.
  • Unifi Premier Card: Unifimoney announced plans for a crypto rewards card with a 2022 target launch. It never came to market. CEO Ben Soppitt noted that "credit cards have proven to be very hard to crack for fintech."
