Cryptocurrency Taxation in Indiana

Key Points

  • Flat Rate Taxation: Indiana applies a flat 3.15% tax rate to all capital gains.
  • Simplified System: Unlike other states, Indiana's capital gains tax is not tied to income levels.
  • No Distinction: Indiana makes no distinction between short-term and long-term gains.
  • Mining Income: Crypto mining is treated as self-employment income and taxed at the same 3.15% rate.

Common Tax Situations

Trading & Investment

All crypto trades, including crypto-to-crypto transactions, are taxable events. The flat 3.15% rate applies to all gains, regardless of holding period or amount.

Tax Planning Tips

While Indiana's flat rate simplifies tax planning, keep detailed records of all transactions for accurate reporting. Consider timing sales to offset gains with losses.
