Coldcard vs Ledger: Overview

Coldcard and Ledger represent opposite philosophies in hardware wallet design. Coldcard focuses exclusively on Bitcoin with maximum security and air-gapped operation, while Ledger prioritizes broad cryptocurrency support and user convenience.

Coldcard, made by Coinkite in Canada since 2011, is the go-to choice for Bitcoin maximalists and security-focused users. Their devices feature dual secure elements, air-gapped operation via microSD and NFC, physical keypads, and advanced features like duress PINs and trick wallets.

Ledger, founded in 2014 in Paris, is the market leader by volume with sleek designs and support for 5,500+ cryptocurrencies. Their devices use secure element chips and the comprehensive Ledger Live app for easy management.

Key Brand Differences

  • Coin Support: Coldcard is Bitcoin-only; Ledger supports 5,500+ cryptocurrencies
  • Air-Gap Capability: Coldcard supports fully air-gapped operation via microSD; Ledger requires USB or Bluetooth connection
  • Open Source: Coldcard firmware is open-source; Ledger firmware is closed-source
  • Secure Elements: Coldcard uses dual secure elements; Ledger uses a single CC EAL5+/EAL6+ certified chip
  • Input Method: Coldcard has physical numeric keypad; Ledger uses buttons or touchscreen
  • Target User: Coldcard is for advanced/technical users; Ledger is more beginner-friendly
  • Price Range: Coldcard MK4 is $168; Ledger ranges from $79 to $399
