Most Private: Bitcoin Wallet

Downloads: 5M
Average Rating: 4.0/5

Bitcoin Wallet (formerly known as 'Schildbach') is one of the oldest and most trusted Android Bitcoin wallets on the Play Store. It does not collect or share app data with third parties and only collects app performance data. All data is encrypted on the device, meaning the developers do not know anything about your wallet contents. Perfect for users concerned about privacy.

Pros

  • Respects user privacy
  • One of the oldest wallets
  • Good for new and advanced users alike

Cons

  • Ugly Interface
  • Initial Set-up Can Use Lots of Cellular Data

Easiest to Use: Edge Wallet

Downloads: 500K
Average Rating: 3.6/5

Edge is a simple, easy-to-understand Bitcoin wallet for Android. It aims to make spending bitcoins simple since it offers a Bitcoin merchant directory and the ability to buy gift cards from within the mobile app! Also, Edge takes advantage of your Android device by allowing you to use your fingerprint to unlock your wallet.

Pros

  • Edge does not know your holdings
  • Fingerprint unlock
  • Simple, intuitive user interface

Cons

  • Edge has limited features
  • Many reports of Edge wallet crashing often

Most Secure: Electrum

Downloads: 500K
Average Rating: 3.7/5

Electrum is an advanced Android Bitcoin wallet for advanced users. Electrum's security and technical features are unmatched by any other wallet on the platform by far. This is for the true nerds who need absolute top-notch security for their coins.

Pros

  • Electrum has an ugly user interface

Most Versatile: Mycelium

Downloads: 1M+
Average Rating: 3.8/5

The Mycelium Bitcoin wallet is another one of the older wallets available on Android, being first released in 2013. Mycelium is great because of its nearly limitless flexibility in terms of where you can access it and how you can manage the interface to your liking. It's not a great wallet for new users though.

Pros

Cons

  • More complicated for new users
  • Ugly user interface

Lightning Network: Blue Wallet

Downloads: 100K
Average Rating: 4.1/5

Blue Wallet is a newer app that has become very popular in recent years. It is most notable for how easy it is to use and its native integration of the Lightning Network, Bitcoin's second layer for cheaper, faster payments. If you want easy access to the Lightning Network, this is the app for you!

Pros

  • Built-in Lightning Network access
  • Beautiful, easy-to-use interface
  • Very Secure

Cons

  • Lacks some of the more advanced features
  • Some reports of freezing and crashing

Built-In Exchange: BitPay

Downloads: 1M+

BitPay Wallet is the wallet created by the well-known crypto exchange and payments provider BitPay. This is one of the most simple wallets on this list. It provides very basic functionality. The only reason you might want to download this wallet is if you want a built-in exchange in the app.

Pros

Cons

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Blockstream's Aqua Available on Android?

Blockstream's simple single-sig wallet called Aqua is now available on Android.

What is Aqua?

Aqua is a single-signature mobile-only wallet, meaning anyone with access to your phone (either physically or remotely) could steal your coins. In exchange for the increased risk, the wallet is easier to use.

Aqua is new, and if you have an Android device, it can be a great addition to Blockstream Green Desktop.

What is Guarda Wallet?

Guarda Wallet is a software wallet that was launched in 2017 with support for Ethereum (ETH). It is available on Android. We do not recommend it as it does not support hardware wallets.

Is Electrum supported on Android?

Electrum wallet is one of the most trusted crypto wallets which stores only Bitcoin. With Electrum release 4.2.1, Android (5.0 and higher) is supported. Note: While Electrum is available on Google Play, make sure to verify the installation of the distributed files using GPG signatures explained on their website. This ensures that the files have not been compromised.

Is BitPay supported on Android?

Yes, BitPay is available on the Google Play Store. We do not recommend it as it does not support hardware wallets and is custodial, meaning you do not hold your coins. BitPay does.

Which wallet is best for investors?

Investors should use an offline or cold wallet, not an Android wallet alone. Android wallets on their own are not very secure, so anyone holding large sums of money in crypto is advised not to use Android wallets without a hardware wallet connected.

What is Exodus wallet?

Exodus is a wallet that supports over a hundred cryptocurrencies. In the beginning, Exodus was originally available only on desktop (Windows, Mac, or Linux), but is now also available for iOS and Android mobile platforms as well. You can also connect it to the Trezor Model T or Trezor One hardware wallets.
